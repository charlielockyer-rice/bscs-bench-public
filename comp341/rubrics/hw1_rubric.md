## Part 4: Oldest and Youngest Names (5 points)

### Question 3: "Recall the plot we made earlier showing total births over time. Do you think that the birth rate can influence the oldest and youngest names we identified? If so, is there a more robust way to choose the 'oldest' and 'youngest' names?"

**Expected Insights:**
- Yes, birth rate affects results because:
  - Higher births in later years = more names have higher median years
  - Names from low-birth-rate eras may appear "older" even if still used
- More robust approaches:
  - Normalize by total births per year (use proportions)
  - Look at peak year instead of median
  - Consider distribution shape (some names have multiple peaks)
  - Use percentile of births rather than absolute median

**Rubric:**
- **Full credit (5 pts)**: Explains how birth rate creates bias AND proposes a reasonable alternative metric
- **Partial credit (3-4 pts)**: Explains the bias OR proposes alternative but not both
- **Minimal credit (1-2 pts)**: Vague acknowledgment of potential issues
- **No credit (0 pts)**: Missing or states birth rate has no influence

## Part 5: Making Predictions (10 points)

### Question 4: "Do the predicted ages match what you have experienced in meeting people with these names? Would you rely on our predictions for guessing the age of people? Why or why not?" (5 pts)

**Expected Insights:**
- Personal experience comparison (subjective, any reasonable answer accepted)
- Limitations of relying on predictions:
  - Names have wide distributions (same name used across generations)
  - Median is a single point estimate, ignores variance
  - Regional and cultural differences in naming
  - Revival of old names (e.g., Emma became popular again)
  - Immigration patterns affect name distributions

**Rubric:**
- **Full credit (5 pts)**: Discusses at least 2 limitations of using name-based age predictions with specific reasoning
- **Partial credit (3-4 pts)**: Mentions limitations but lacks depth or specificity
- **Minimal credit (1-2 pts)**: Says "no" without meaningful explanation
- **No credit (0 pts)**: Missing or says predictions are reliable without caveats

### Question 5: "Based on your understanding of the data and means versus medians, what difference does choosing 'old' and 'young' names using median year or mean year make? What if you chose based on mode (the total births) or another metric? How do you think these choices would affect the results?" (5 pts)

**Expected Insights:**
- **Median vs Mean:**
  - Median: robust to outliers, represents "typical" birth year
  - Mean: sensitive to distribution tails, pulled by recent/old usage
  - Skewed distributions: mean and median diverge
- **Mode considerations:**
  - Mode = most common year (peak popularity)
  - May miss names with multiple popularity peaks
  - Better for "when was this name most popular"
- **Effects on results:**
  - Different metrics may rank names differently
  - Names with long tails vs concentrated peaks behave differently
  - Cyclic names (come back in fashion) are tricky

**Rubric:**
- **Full credit (5 pts)**: Explains differences between median/mean/mode with examples of how they'd affect results
- **Partial credit (3-4 pts)**: Understands differences but doesn't connect to specific effects on name rankings
- **Minimal credit (1-2 pts)**: Mentions the metrics exist but doesn't explain implications
- **No credit (0 pts)**: Missing or confuses the metrics
